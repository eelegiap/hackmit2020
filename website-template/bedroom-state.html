Noise and Environment

Even with access to all of the necessary technologies (high-speed internet, laptop, etc.), students cannot effectively study in difficult working environments. Living with too many people in one home often leads to space constraints and limited individual access to bandwidth, making it difficult to work and focus. The effects of a noisy environment were felt by students this past spring and underestimated by parents: a survey by BrightBytes found that students report being less likely than teachers to have a consistently quiet place to work, and that parents are much more likely to say that their children have a quiet environment than the students themselves.



More about our analysis

Data on factors such as noise and crowdedness of a household does not exist in a comprehensive manner. As a proxy estimate for noise and crowdedness, we created an index involving number of bedrooms and number of people in one household. In general, if there are n people living in a household, we assume that there is not enough room for each person to have a quiet and private workspace if there are less than n-1 bedrooms.
