Lack of Internet Access: All

The “digital divide” refers to the gap between those who do and those who do not have access to technology. In the context of COVID-19, the digital divide exacerbates and will continue to exacerbate differences in academic outcomes between those who have better technological access to essential tools such as the internet. Not surprisingly, having some access to internet is crucial to the most basic of academic tasks in the age of remote learning, such as accessing course materials and submitting homework. Principals reported home internet access as the top issue limiting distance instruction. Moreover, unlike in years prior to COVID-19, high-speed internet access has been especially crucial to attending class through video conferencing platforms that aim to replicate in-person learning environments.
