Food Insecurity

Affecting an estimated 11% of American households in 2018, food insecurity is the disruption of food intake resulting from a lack of money and other resources. School lunch is critical to the health and well-being of students, especially for those affected by food insecurity. In fact, for some children, school meals may be the only meal they have on a given day. When schools transitioned to a remote format in the spring of 2020, many vulnerable students were left without school meals. By some estimates, only 15% of students who qualify for free or reduced-price school meals have been getting them. Some school districts have created a pick-up system for school meals, but the need for a parent to come to the pick-up site is a barrier for families where parents are busy working or have disabilities. As remote education continues, food insecurity is expected to continue to pose a challenge to students in food-insecure households.
