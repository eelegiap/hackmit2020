Lack of Computer Access

Computers, the most integral tool for remote learning, aren't a given for all families. Survey data reveals why such discrepancies across income might exist: one survey found that about a third of teachers in high-poverty schools described access to computers as a major limitation to class participation. A Pew Research Center poll conducted in early April 2020 revealed that roughly one in five parents with homebound school-aged children say it is very likely or somewhat likely their children will not be able complete their schoolwork because they do not have access to a computer at home (21 percent), and about three in ten parents (29 percent) report that it is at least somewhat likely their children will have to do their schoolwork on a cell phone.


In effect, the absence of computers at home can have disastrous results for students. The inability to log onto online classes contributes to chronic absenteeism and decreased learning among students. With public libraries temporarily shuttered and school resources out of reach, students must rely on the technological tools they can find at home. In result, the switch to online learning during COVID exposes the regional, racial, and socioeconomic inequalities surrounding access to technology, and students already at risk can be left behind.
